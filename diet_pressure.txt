    Low-sodium foods: Foods that are high in sodium can increase blood pressure, so it's important to limit your intake of processed foods, canned foods, and fast foods.
    Fruits and vegetables: Eating a diet rich in fruits and vegetables can help lower blood pressure. Aim for at least 4-5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day.
    Whole grains: Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and quinoa can help lower blood pressure.
    Lean protein: Choose lean protein sources such as fish, chicken, and tofu.
    Low-fat dairy: Dairy products such as milk and yogurt can help lower blood pressure, but it's important to choose low-fat options.
    Healthy fats: Include healthy fats in your diet such as avocado, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.
    Reduce alcohol consumption: Consuming too much alcohol can raise blood pressure, so it's important to limit alcohol intake or avoid it altogether.
    Limit added sugars: Foods and drinks high in added sugars can contribute to weight gain and high blood pressure.
    Eating foods rich in magnesium: Foods such as nuts, seeds, legumes, and leafy greens are good sources of magnesium, which helps regulate blood pressure.
    Eating foods rich in potassium: Potassium-rich foods such as bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach, and tomatoes can help lower blood pressure.
